Meet Ashlyn Hall

Available

Meet Ashlyn, the creative force behind our group's most vibrant moments and artistic endeavors. As a founding member of the Pickles and a proud member of the Cool Kids Club, Ashlyn brings her artistic vision and innovative spirit to every gathering, transforming ordinary activities into extraordinary experiences through her unique creative perspective.

About Me

I've always seen the world through a creative lens, finding beauty and inspiration in the most unexpected places. As a former set designer turned color theorist, I'm fascinated by how colors interact with each other and how they can evoke different emotions. My workshops with the Pickles group focus on helping everyone discover their own creative voice, whether through photography, painting, or mixed media projects. I believe that creativity isn't just about making art—it's about approaching life with curiosity, openness, and a willingness to experiment. When I'm not leading our creative sessions, I'm usually sketching in my watercolor journal, exploring new artistic techniques, or hunting for unique materials to incorporate into my next project.

Fun Facts

  • Created a mural series that was featured in three local galleries
  • Designed sets for an award-winning independent film
  • Can identify over 50 different color shades by name without reference
  • Makes handmade paper from recycled materials for special art projects

How I Joined Pickles

My journey with the Pickles began during an outdoor art festival where I was showcasing my mixed media pieces. Noah and Chance stopped by my booth, and we instantly connected over our shared love for creative expression and outdoor adventures. They were planning a weekend camping trip that would include some nature photography and invited me to join them to provide an artistic perspective. I remember being hesitant at first—joining a group of strangers for a weekend in the wilderness seemed like quite the leap! But something about their genuine enthusiasm and the way they described their friend group made me take that chance. That weekend turned out to be transformative. As we hiked through canyons and captured the changing light on the landscape, I found myself surrounded by people who appreciated both art and adventure in equal measure. By the campfire that night, as we shared stories and dreams, I knew I'd found my tribe. Being one of the founding members of Pickles has been such a gift—watching our creative community grow and evolve while maintaining that special connection that brought us together in the first place.
