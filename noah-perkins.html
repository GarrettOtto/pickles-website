Meet Noah Perkins

Available

Noah Perkins is the adventurous spirit of the Pickles friend group, always ready to lead the next exciting expedition or organize a friendly competition. With boundless energy and a passion for the outdoors, Noah brings enthusiasm and spontaneity to every gathering. His natural leadership abilities and genuine love for bringing people together have made him one of the founding pillars of our community, constantly inspiring others to step outside their comfort zones and embrace new experiences.

About Me

I've always been drawn to adventure and the great outdoors. Growing up near the mountains, I developed a deep appreciation for nature and physical activities at an early age. I'm passionate about sports of all kinds, particularly pickleball, hiking, and rock climbing. When I'm not organizing our next group adventure, you can find me exploring new trails, planning tournaments, or simply enjoying the outdoors. I believe that shared experiences create the strongest bonds, which is why I'm constantly looking for new activities that bring our friend group together. My philosophy is simple: life is meant to be lived actively, and the best memories are created when we challenge ourselves alongside good friends.

Fun Facts

  • I've hiked in 15 different national parks
  • I organize a weekly pickleball tournament
  • I once spent a month backpacking through the Rockies
  • I've visited 27 states and have a goal to see all 50

How I Joined Pickles

The Pickles group actually began with a simple idea I had during my freshman year of college. I had just moved to a new city and was eager to build meaningful connections beyond the typical college social scene. I met Chance during orientation week, and we immediately bonded over our shared love for outdoor activities. We started organizing small weekend hikes and pickleball games, inviting new people each time. What began as casual gatherings quickly evolved into something more significant as our circle expanded. The name "Pickles" came about during one of our early pickleball tournaments - it was a playful nod to the sport that initially brought many of us together, but it stuck as our group identity grew beyond just sports. I'm incredibly proud of how our community has evolved over the years, welcoming diverse personalities while maintaining the core values of adventure, friendship, and genuine connection. Being a founding member of Pickles has been one of the most rewarding experiences of my life, watching it grow from a small circle of friends into the vibrant community it is today.
